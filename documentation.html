Research Algorithms

We studied a variety of cryptographic algorithms across difficulty levels, analyzing their structure and internet applications.

  • Caesar Cipher (Simple) - Basic obfuscation; used in educational demos.
  • Playfair Cipher (Simple) - Historical digraph substitution method; good for manual encryption understanding.
  • Vigenère Cipher (Intermediate) - Polyalphabetic method for improving resistance to frequency analysis.
  • Hill Cipher (Intermediate) - Matrix-based encryption introducing linear algebra into cryptography.
  • AES (Advanced) - Symmetric key encryption used in web protocols and data security.
  • RSA (Advanced) - Public-key algorithm for secure key exchanges and digital signatures.

Algorithm Selection & Implementation

Algorithms were chosen based on their educational value, complexity, and modern relevance. Implementation focused on modular JavaScript functions and frontend integration.
